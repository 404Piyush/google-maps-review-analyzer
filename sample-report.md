# Customer Feedback Analysis

## 1. Executive Summary

Customers overwhelmingly praise the restaurant for its exceptional **service** and **food quality**, which together account for over 60% of positive mentions. Sentiment is strongly positive at **78%**, with only **12%** of reviews expressing dissatisfaction. The most significant pain point is **wait time**, cited predominantly in negative reviews. The business is well-positioned as a value-driven dining destination with strong customer loyalty, though operational efficiency during peak hours presents a clear opportunity for improvement.

## 3. Deep Dive: Key Themes

### What's Working Well

1. **Exceptional staff service** — Reviewers consistently call out warm, attentive service as a reason to return. *Quote:* "Loved the paneer tikka and the staff remembered my name. Great experience overall."
2. **Inviting ambience** — The interior design and atmosphere are widely praised, especially for couples and families. *Quote:* "Cozy place with friendly servers."
3. **Menu variety & quality** — A diverse menu with consistently good execution keeps customers coming back. *Quote:* "Best biryani in the area. Menu variety is impressive."

### Areas for Improvement

1. **Wait time during peak hours** — The single largest source of negative reviews. Tables reserved in advance still incur 30–45 minute delays. *Quote:* "Waited 45 minutes for a table even with a reservation."
2. **Perceived value for price** — Portion size vs. cost is the second-most cited concern, particularly for first-time visitors. *Quote:* "Portions could be a bit larger for the price."
3. **Noise levels** — A handful of reviews mention the dining room getting loud when full, detracting from the otherwise praised ambience. *Quote:* "The place was clean but quite loud."

## 4. Actionable Recommendations

- **Stagger reservations by 15 minutes** during Friday/Saturday peak — projected to eliminate ~70% of wait-time complaints.
- **Introduce a value-size or share menu** at the same price point — addresses portion perception without reducing margins.
- **Add soft sound-absorbing panels** in the main dining room — low-capex fix for the noise complaint cluster.
- **Launch a "regulars" loyalty program** — capitalize on the already-strong repeat customer base identified in positive reviews.
